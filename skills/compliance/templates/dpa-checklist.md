## 1. Controller / Processor Roles

- [ ] The DPA clearly identifies your organisation as **Data Controller**
- [ ] The DPA clearly identifies the vendor as **Data Processor**
- [ ] If the vendor also acts as a controller for some purposes, this is clearly distinguished
- [ ] Roles are consistent with the actual data flows

---

## 2. Purpose and Legal Basis

- [ ] The purpose(s) of processing are explicitly stated
- [ ] Processing is limited to what is necessary for the stated purposes
- [ ] The controller's lawful basis for processing is documented (Art. 6 / Art. 9)
- [ ] The vendor will not process data for purposes beyond those specified without prior written consent

> [List the specific processing purposes]

- [ ] Estimated volume of data subjects is recorded

- [ ] Encryption at rest: [standard specified, e.g. AES-256]

- [ ] Access controls: role-based access, MFA for privileged access
- [ ] Security certifications: [ISO 27001 / SOC 2 Type II / equivalent]
  - Certificate date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
  - Expiry: [YYYY-MM-DD]
- [ ] Penetration testing: [frequency and most recent date]
- [ ] Security incident response: vendor has documented incident response process

---

## 7. Data Breach Notification

- [ ] Vendor commits to notifying you of a breach affecting your data within **[72 hours]** (or sooner) of becoming aware
- [ ] Notification must include: nature of breach, data categories affected, approximate number of data subjects, likely consequences, measures taken/proposed
- [ ] Contact for breach notifications is specified: [name/email/mechanism]
- [ ] Notification timeline is consistent with your own 72-hour obligation to the DPA

---

## 8. Data Subject Rights

- [ ] Vendor will assist you in fulfilling data subject rights requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection)
- [ ] Response time for assistance: [X business days]
- [ ] Process for submitting rights requests to the vendor is documented

---

## 9. International Transfers

- [ ] All countries where data is processed or stored are identified
- [ ] For non-EEA / non-adequate countries, a valid transfer mechanism is in place:
  - [ ] EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs — 2021 version) incorporated
  - [ ] Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
  - [ ] Adequacy decision (list the country)
- [ ] Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) completed if SCCs are used with high-risk countries

---

## 10. Audit Rights

- [ ] You have the right to audit the vendor's compliance with the DPA
- [ ] Audit mechanism is specified: [direct audit / third-party audit / certification sharing]
- [ ] Vendor must provide information necessary to demonstrate compliance (Art. 28(3)(h))

---

## 11. Termination and Data Return

- [ ] DPA specifies what happens to data at contract end
- [ ] You can choose between: data returned in a portable format OR secure deletion
- [ ] Deletion/return must be completed within [X days] of termination
- [ ] Vendor provides written confirmation of deletion on request
